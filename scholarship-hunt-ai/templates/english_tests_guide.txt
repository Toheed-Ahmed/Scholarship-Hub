ENGLISH PROFICIENCY TESTS GUIDE

If your target scholarship or university is in an English-speaking country or the program relies on English as the medium of instruction, you will most likely need one of the following:

1. IELTS (International English Language Testing System):
- Usually, a minimum overall band score of 6.5 (with no sub-band below 6.0) is required for Master's programs.
- Result validity: 2 Years.

2. TOEFL iBT (Test of English as a Foreign Language):
- Standard requirement ranges from 80 to 90+ out of 120.
- Result validity: 2 Years.

3. ENGLISH PROFICIENCY CERTIFICATE (EPC):
- For certain scholarships (often in Europe, China, or South Korea), you may substitute an official testing score with a letter from your previous university stating that your Bachelor's degree was taught entirely in English.
- *Always verify with the official scholarship guidelines whether an EPC is accepted in place of IELTS/TOEFL.*

TIPS FOR THE TEST:
- Book your test at least 2 months before your scholarship deadline.
- Practice daily on platforms like IELTS Liz, Cambridge Official Guide, or Magoosh.
- Ensure you upload a clear scan of the TRF (Test Report Form) provided to you.
